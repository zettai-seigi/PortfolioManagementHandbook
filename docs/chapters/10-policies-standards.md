## Portfolio Management Policy

### Policy Statement

*All IT investments shall be managed through the IT Portfolio Management process to ensure strategic alignment, optimize resource utilization, and maximize business value.*

### Policy Scope

| In Scope | Out of Scope |
|----------|--------------|
| IT projects and programs | Individual operational tasks |
| Application investments | Hardware procurement (< threshold) |
| Infrastructure investments | Routine maintenance |
| Service improvements | Emergency fixes |
| Business applications | Personal productivity tools |

### Policy Principles

| Principle | Statement |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Strategic Alignment** | All investments must demonstrate alignment to organizational strategy |
| **Business Justification** | All investments must have approved business cases |
| **Prioritization** | Investments shall be prioritized using consistent criteria |
| **Governance** | Investments shall follow established governance processes |
| **Transparency** | Portfolio information shall be visible to stakeholders |
| **Accountability** | Business sponsors are accountable for benefits realization |
| **Optimization** | The portfolio shall be actively managed to maximize value |

## Investment Standards

### Investment Thresholds

| Threshold | Amount | Approval Authority | Requirements |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Minor** | < $50K | PMO | Brief justification |
| **Small** | $50K - $100K | Investment Board | Business case (simplified) |
| **Medium** | $100K - $500K | Investment Board | Full business case |
| **Large** | $500K - $1M | Steering Committee | Full business case + detailed plan |
| **Major** | > $1M | Steering Committee + Executive | Full business case + board briefing |

### Business Case Requirements

| Investment Size | Required Elements |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Minor** | Problem statement, solution, rough cost |
| **Small** | Problem, solution, benefits estimate, cost estimate |
| **Medium** | Full business case template (all sections) |
| **Large** | Full business case + detailed plan + risk assessment |
| **Major** | Full business case + detailed plan + external review |

### Financial Analysis Standards

| Metric | Requirement | Threshold |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **NPV** | Required for all > $100K | > $0 |
| **ROI** | Required for all > $100K | > organizational hurdle rate |
| **Payback Period** | Required for all > $100K | < 3 years |
| **IRR** | Recommended for > $500K | > cost of capital |
| **Sensitivity Analysis** | Required for > $1M | Document key assumptions |

## Scoring Standards

### Scoring Criteria

| Dimension | Weight | Scale | Standard |
|-----------|--------|-------|----------|
| **Business Value** | 40% | 0-5 | Per scoring framework |
| **Risk** | 30% | 0-5 | Per risk assessment criteria |
| **Cost** | 30% | 0-5 | Per cost scoring criteria |

### Scoring Process Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **Independence** | Scorers complete individual scores before discussion |
| **Documentation** | Scoring rationale documented |
| **Calibration** | Annual scoring calibration session |
| **Conflict of Interest** | Scorers with conflicts must recuse |
| **Quorum** | Minimum 5 scorers required |

### Priority Classification

| Priority | Score Range | Definition |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| **P1 - Critical** | > 5.0 | Must do, fund immediately |
| **P2 - High** | 3.5 - 5.0 | Should do, fund this cycle |
| **P3 - Medium** | 2.0 - 3.5 | Could do, consider next cycle |
| **P4 - Low** | 0 - 2.0 | Defer |
| **P5 - Do Not Fund** | < 0 | Reject |

## Project Standards

### Gate Standards

| Gate | Required Deliverables | Approval Authority |
|------|----------------------|-------------------|
| **Gate 1: Idea** | Concept paper, initial estimate | PMO |
| **Gate 2: Business Case** | Full business case, financial analysis | Investment Board |
| **Gate 3: Plan** | Detailed plan, budget, resources | Steering Committee (> $500K) |
| **Gate 4: Execute** | Readiness checklist | Project governance |
| **Gate 5: Deploy** | Go-live checklist, business sign-off | Project governance |
| **Gate 6: Close** | Final report, lessons learned | PMO |

### Project Reporting Standards

| Report | Frequency | Content | Audience |
|--------|-----------|---------|----------|
| **Status Report** | Weekly | Progress, issues, risks | Project governance |
| **Portfolio Status** | Monthly | Health, milestones, metrics | Steering Committee |
| **Benefits Report** | Quarterly | Realization vs. plan | Steering Committee |
| **Final Report** | At closure | Outcomes, lessons learned | PMO |

### Status Indicator Standards

| Status | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| **Green** | On track: schedule, budget, scope all within tolerance |
| **Yellow** | At risk: One metric outside tolerance, corrective action underway |
| **Red** | Off track: Multiple metrics outside tolerance, escalation needed |

### Tolerance Standards

| Metric | Green | Yellow | Red |
|--------|-------|--------|-----|
| **Schedule** | On time | < 10% delay | > 10% delay |
| **Budget** | On budget | < 10% over | > 10% over |
| **Scope** | Complete | < 10% reduction | > 10% reduction |

## Benefits Standards

### Benefits Definition Standards

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| **Benefit Statement** | Clear, specific, measurable |
| **Baseline** | Current state measured |
| **Target** | Expected future state quantified |
| **Measurement Method** | How benefit will be measured |
| **Measurement Frequency** | How often measurement occurs |
| **Benefit Owner** | Named individual accountable |
| **Realization Timeline** | When benefit expected |

### Benefits Categories

| Category | Examples | Measurement |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| **Revenue** | New revenue, increased sales | $ revenue |
| **Cost Savings** | Reduced costs, efficiency | $ savings |
| **Cost Avoidance** | Prevented future costs | $ avoided |
| **Productivity** | FTE savings, time reduction | FTE or hours |
| **Quality** | Error reduction, satisfaction | % improvement |
| **Risk Reduction** | Mitigated risks | Risk score |
| **Strategic** | Capability enablement | Qualitative |

### Benefits Tracking Standards

| Milestone | Timing | Activities |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Baseline** | Before project | Measure current state |
| **Initial** | 3 months post-go-live | First measurement |
| **Interim** | 6 months post-go-live | Progress assessment |
| **Final** | 12 months post-go-live | Full realization assessment |
| **Close** | 18 months post-go-live | Close benefits tracking |

## Compliance and Audit

### Compliance Requirements

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Policy Compliance** | All investments follow portfolio policy |
| **Financial Controls** | Investments comply with financial policies |
| **Governance Compliance** | Decisions made by authorized bodies |
| **Documentation** | Required artifacts complete and archived |
| **Audit Trail** | Decision history maintained |

### Audit Checkpoints

| Checkpoint | Verification |
|------------|--------------|
| **Business Case Approval** | Approved by appropriate authority |
| **Scoring Documentation** | Scores and rationale documented |
| **Gate Approvals** | Each gate properly approved |
| **Change Approvals** | Changes follow change process |
| **Benefits Tracking** | Benefits measured and reported |
| **Closure Documentation** | Final report and lessons learned |

### Audit Evidence

| Evidence Type | Retention |
|---------------|-----------|
| Business cases | 7 years |
| Approval records | 7 years |
| Scoring documentation | 5 years |
| Status reports | 3 years |
| Final reports | 7 years |
| Benefits reports | 7 years |

## Policy Exceptions

### Exception Process

1. **Request Exception:** Submit exception request to Portfolio Manager
2. **Review:** PMO assesses request against criteria
3. **Recommendation:** PMO recommends approve/deny
4. **Decision:** Appropriate authority decides
5. **Document:** Exception and rationale documented
6. **Monitor:** Track exception expiration

### Exception Authority

| Exception Type | Authority |
|----------------|-----------|
| Minor process deviation | Portfolio Manager |
| Threshold deviation | Investment Board |
| Policy exception | Steering Committee |
| Governance exception | Process Owner |

## Key Takeaways

- **Policies** establish governance principles and requirements
- **Standards** define consistent criteria and thresholds
- **Procedures** provide detailed how-to guidance
- **Compliance** ensures adherence to requirements
- **Exceptions** allow flexibility with appropriate controls

---

## Summary

Portfolio management policies and standards provide the foundation for consistent, effective governance. Clear investment thresholds, business case requirements, scoring standards, and benefits tracking ensure all investments are properly justified and managed. Compliance mechanisms and audit checkpoints verify adherence to policies while exception processes provide appropriate flexibility.
