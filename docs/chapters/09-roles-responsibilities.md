# Chapter 9: Roles and Responsibilities

## Learning Objectives

After completing this chapter, you will be able to:
- Identify key roles in portfolio management
- Understand responsibilities for each role
- Define skills and competencies required
- Apply RACI for portfolio activities
- Build an effective portfolio management team

---

## Key Portfolio Management Roles

### Role Overview

| Role | Primary Focus | Reports To |
|------|---------------|------------|
| **Portfolio Management Process Owner** | Process design, improvement | CIO |
| **Portfolio Manager** | Day-to-day portfolio operations | Process Owner |
| **Portfolio Analyst** | Analysis, reporting, data | Portfolio Manager |
| **Business Relationship Manager** | Business-IT alignment | CIO/Business |
| **Business Sponsor** | Initiative ownership | Business Leadership |
| **Project/Program Manager** | Initiative execution | PMO |

---

## Portfolio Management Process Owner

### Role Definition

The Process Owner has overall accountability for the portfolio management process, ensuring it delivers value and aligns with organizational objectives.

### Responsibilities

| Area | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|
| **Process** | Design, implement, and improve portfolio management process |
| **Governance** | Establish governance structure and decision rights |
| **Policy** | Define portfolio policies and standards |
| **Integration** | Ensure integration with other IT processes |
| **Performance** | Monitor and improve process performance |
| **Sponsorship** | Champion portfolio management across organization |

### Authority

- Approve portfolio management policies
- Approve governance structure changes
- Escalate strategic issues to CIO
- Approve process improvements
- Set portfolio management targets

### Skills and Competencies

| Skill | Level |
|-------|-------|
| Strategic thinking | Expert |
| Process management | Expert |
| Stakeholder management | Expert |
| Financial acumen | Advanced |
| Change management | Advanced |
| Leadership | Expert |

## Portfolio Manager

### Role Definition

The Portfolio Manager is responsible for day-to-day portfolio management operations, analysis, and reporting.

### Responsibilities

| Area | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|
| **Demand Management** | Manage investment intake and qualification |
| **Analysis** | Conduct portfolio analysis and scenario modeling |
| **Prioritization** | Facilitate scoring and prioritization |
| **Reporting** | Produce portfolio dashboards and reports |
| **Governance Support** | Prepare and support governance meetings |
| **Optimization** | Recommend portfolio balancing actions |
| **Benefits** | Track and report benefits realization |
| **Communication** | Communicate portfolio status to stakeholders |

### Authority

- Approve intake of investment proposals
- Make recommendations to governance bodies
- Escalate issues and risks
- Manage PMO team
- Approve routine portfolio changes

### Skills and Competencies

| Skill | Level |
|-------|-------|
| Portfolio management | Expert |
| Financial analysis | Advanced |
| Analytical thinking | Expert |
| Communication | Expert |
| Facilitation | Advanced |
| Tool proficiency | Advanced |
| Stakeholder management | Advanced |

### Typical Activities

| Frequency | Activities |
|-----------|------------|
| **Daily** | Monitor portfolio status, respond to queries |
| **Weekly** | Update dashboards, prepare for governance meetings |
| **Bi-weekly** | Investment Review Board preparation and facilitation |
| **Monthly** | Steering Committee reporting, portfolio analysis |
| **Quarterly** | Portfolio rebalancing analysis, benefits review |
| **Annual** | Strategic portfolio planning, process improvement |

### Role Definition

The Business Sponsor is the business owner of an initiative, accountable for business case and benefits realization.

### Responsibilities

| Area | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|

| **Benefits** | Define and realize expected benefits |
| **Requirements** | Provide business requirements |
| **Decisions** | Make business decisions for initiative |
| **Resources** | Provide business resources and support |
| **Change** | Champion organizational change |
| **Accountability** | Accountable for initiative success |

### Authority

- Approve business requirements
- Make business trade-off decisions
- Approve go-live readiness
- Own benefits realization

### Skills and Competencies

| Skill | Level |
|-------|-------|
| Business leadership | Expert |
| Decision making | Expert |
| Change management | Advanced |
| Financial acumen | Advanced |

| Stakeholder management | Advanced |

## RACI Matrix

### Portfolio Management RACI

| Activity | Process Owner | Portfolio Manager | Portfolio Analyst | BRM | Business Sponsor | Project Manager |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|-------------------|-----|------------------|-----------------|
| **Demand Management** |
| Collect demands | I | A | R | R | R | C |
| Qualify requests | C | A | R | C | C | I |
| **Portfolio Definition** |
| Develop business case | C | C | C | C | A/R | R |
| Score initiatives | C | A | R | C | C | C |
| **Portfolio Analysis** |
| Analyze portfolio | I | A | R | I | I | I |
| Model scenarios | I | A | R | C | I | I |
| **Portfolio Optimization** |
| Prioritize initiatives | A | R | R | C | C | I |
| Balance portfolio | A | R | R | C | I | I |
| **Portfolio Governance** |
| Approve investments | A | R | C | C | C | I |
| Manage changes | A | R | C | C | C | R |
| **Portfolio Monitoring** |
| Track status | I | A | R | I | I | R |
| Report performance | I | A | R | I | I | R |
| Track benefits | C | A | R | C | R | C |

**Legend:** R=Responsible, A=Accountable, C=Consulted, I=Informed

## Team Structure

|------|-----|-------|

|------|-----|-------|

## Key Takeaways

- **Process Owner** has accountability for portfolio management capability
- **Portfolio Manager** leads day-to-day operations
- **Business Sponsors** own business cases and benefits
- **RACI clarity** prevents gaps and overlaps
- **Right team size** depends on organization scale
- **Skills development** enables maturity growth

---

## Summary

Effective portfolio management requires clearly defined roles with appropriate skills and authority. The RACI matrix ensures everyone understands their responsibilities without gaps or overlaps. Team size should match organizational scale, and skills development ensures the team can mature portfolio management capabilities over time.
