# Chapter 7: Portfolio Balancing

## Learning Objectives

After completing this chapter, you will be able to:
- Understand the importance of portfolio balance
- Apply category allocation targets to portfolio decisions
- Balance risk across the portfolio
- Optimize resource allocation
- Conduct portfolio rationalization exercises

---

## Why Portfolio Balance Matters

### The Balanced Portfolio Principle

A balanced portfolio:
- **Diversifies risk** across multiple initiatives
- **Aligns investments** to strategic priorities
- **Optimizes resources** across competing demands
- **Ensures sustainability** of long-term value creation

### Consequences of Imbalance

| Imbalance | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| Too much Transform | High risk, resource strain, execution failures |
| Too much Run | Stagnation, competitive disadvantage |
| Too few quick wins | Loss of momentum, stakeholder fatigue |
| Too many initiatives | Resource dilution, poor delivery |
| All high-risk projects | Portfolio volatility, failure concentration |

---

## Portfolio Balance Dimensions

### Key Balance Dimensions

| Dimension | Description | Balance Objective |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Strategic** | Transform/Grow/Run/Comply mix | Align to strategy |
| **Risk** | High/Medium/Low risk distribution | Manage overall risk |
| **Timeline** | Short/Medium/Long-term initiatives | Continuous delivery |
| **Resource** | Skill and capacity utilization | Optimize utilization |
| **Innovation** | Core/Adjacent/Transformational | Foster innovation |

---

## Strategic Balance

### Category Allocation Targets

Define target allocation based on strategic priorities:

| Strategy Type | Transform | Grow | Run | Comply |
|---------------|-----------|------|-----|--------|
| **Growth-focused** | 25% | 35% | 30% | 10% |
| **Efficiency-focused** | 15% | 20% | 55% | 10% |
| **Transformation** | 35% | 25% | 25% | 15% |
| **Stability-focused** | 10% | 20% | 55% | 15% |
| **Balanced** | 20% | 25% | 45% | 10% |

### Setting Category Targets

**Factors to Consider:**
1. Current strategic priorities
2. Market conditions and competition
3. Technical debt levels
4. Regulatory requirements
5. Resource availability
6. Risk appetite

### Category Balance Assessment

| Category | Target | Current | Variance | Action |
|----------|--------|---------|----------|--------|
| Transform | 20% | 12% | -8% | Increase |
| Grow | 25% | 18% | -7% | Increase |
| Run | 45% | 58% | +13% | Reduce |
| Comply | 10% | 12% | +2% | Acceptable |

## Risk Balance

### Risk Distribution Targets

| Risk Level | Target | Description |
|------------|--------|-------------|
| **High Risk** | 15-25% | Breakthrough potential, uncertain outcomes |
| **Medium Risk** | 40-50% | Moderate uncertainty, manageable challenges |
| **Low Risk** | 30-40% | Predictable outcomes, proven approaches |

### Managing Risk Balance

**When High Risk is Over Target:**
- Defer or stage high-risk initiatives
- Require more rigorous planning
- Add quick wins to offset

**When Low Risk is Under Target:**
- Add proven, predictable initiatives
- Include operational improvements
- Fast-track low-risk proposals

## Timeline Balance

### Timeline Distribution

| Horizon | Duration | Target | Purpose |
|---------|----------|--------|---------|
| **Short-term** | 0-6 months | 30-40% | Quick wins, momentum |
| **Medium-term** | 6-12 months | 35-45% | Substantive improvements |
| **Long-term** | 12+ months | 20-30% | Strategic transformation |

### Timeline Balance Principles

**Continuous Delivery:**
- Avoid having all initiatives complete simultaneously
- Stagger completions for steady flow of wins
- Balance resource peaks and valleys

**Quick Wins:**
- Include short-term initiatives for visible progress
- Build stakeholder confidence and momentum
- Demonstrate portfolio value regularly

## Resource Balance

### Capacity-Based Portfolio Sizing

**Step 1: Determine Available Capacity**

| Resource Type | Available | Utilization Target | Effective Capacity |
|--------------|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Internal FTE | 100 | 80% | 80 FTE |
| Budget | $10M | 95% | $9.5M |
| Vendor capacity | 50 FTE | 90% | 45 FTE |

**Step 2: Size Portfolio to Capacity**

| Category | Capacity % | FTE | Budget |
|----------|------------|-----|--------|
| Transform | 20% | 16 | $1.9M |
| Grow | 25% | 20 | $2.4M |
| Run | 45% | 36 | $4.3M |
| Comply | 10% | 8 | $0.9M |
| **Total** | 100% | 80 | $9.5M |

### Resource Utilization Targets

| Metric | Target | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Overall utilization | 80-85% | Buffer for emergencies |
| Individual utilization | 75-85% | Avoid burnout |
| Critical resource utilization | < 70% | Reduce bottleneck risk |

### Avoiding Resource Concentration

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Over-reliance on key individuals | Cross-train, distribute expertise |
| Single vendor dependency | Multi-vendor strategy |
| Skill concentration | Skill development plan |
| Geographic concentration | Distributed team options |

## Portfolio Rationalization

### When to Rationalize

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Portfolio exceeds capacity | Reduce active initiatives |
| Category imbalance | Rebalance allocation |
| Underperforming initiatives | Cancel or restructure |
| Strategic shift | Realign portfolio |
| Budget reduction | Prioritize and cut |
| Major completion | Rebalance resources |

### Rationalization Process

**Step 1: Assess Current Portfolio**
- List all active initiatives
- Calculate total resource demand
- Identify category distribution
- Assess performance status

**Step 2: Identify Rationalization Candidates**

| Candidate Type | Criteria |
|----------------|----------|
| **Low Priority** | P3 or P4 priority score |
| **Low Progress** | < 30% complete, rescope possible |
| **Poor Performance** | Red status, repeated delays |
| **Low Utilization** | Resources underutilized |
| **Duplicate** | Similar scope to other initiatives |
| **Outdated** | Business need changed |

**Step 3: Evaluate Options**

| Option | Description | Criteria |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Continue** | Proceed as planned | High priority, good performance |
| **Accelerate** | Add resources, compress schedule | Critical need, capacity available |
| **Defer** | Place on hold | Good idea, wrong time |
| **Descope** | Reduce scope | Core value can be delivered faster |
| **Combine** | Merge with related initiative | Synergies available |
| **Cancel** | Terminate | Low value, poor outlook |

**Step 4: Execute Decisions**
- Communicate decisions to stakeholders
- Reallocate resources
- Update portfolio tracking
- Document rationale

### Rationalization Outcomes

| Initiative | Decision | Rationale | Resource Impact |
|------------|----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Project A | Continue | P1 priority, on track | No change |
| Project B | Defer | P3, capacity constraint | Release 5 FTE |
| Project C | Cancel | Business need changed | Release 3 FTE |
| Project D | Descope | Core value achievable | Reduce 2 FTE |
| Proposal E | Fund | P2, resources now available | Assign 8 FTE |

## Portfolio Rebalancing

### Rebalancing Cadence

| Review | Frequency | Scope |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| **Health Check** | Monthly | Status, risks, issues |
| **Rebalancing** | Quarterly | Category mix, capacity |
| **Strategic Review** | Annual | Full portfolio planning |
| **Event-Triggered** | As needed | Major changes |

### Quarterly Rebalancing Process

1. **Assess Current State**
   - Update portfolio inventory
   - Calculate category allocation
   - Assess resource utilization
   - Review performance metrics

2. **Compare to Targets**
   - Category targets vs. actual
   - Capacity vs. demand
   - Performance vs. plan

3. **Identify Adjustments**
   - Initiatives to accelerate/defer
   - Resources to reallocate
   - New initiatives to fund

4. **Present to Steering Committee**
   - Portfolio status
   - Recommended adjustments
   - Resource implications

5. **Execute Approved Changes**
   - Implement decisions
   - Communicate to stakeholders
   - Update tracking

## Key Takeaways

- **Portfolio balance** is essential for sustainable value creation
- **Category targets** guide investment allocation to strategy
- **Risk balance** prevents portfolio volatility
- **Resource capacity** determines portfolio size
- **Rationalization** optimizes portfolio composition
- **Regular rebalancing** maintains alignment over time

---

## Summary

Portfolio balancing ensures IT investments are appropriately distributed across strategic categories, risk levels, timelines, and resources. By setting targets and regularly assessing portfolio composition, organizations can maintain strategic alignment while managing risk and optimizing resource utilization. Rationalization exercises help remove underperforming initiatives and free resources for higher-value opportunities.
